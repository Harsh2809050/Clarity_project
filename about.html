Why I Started This

India has a problem that doesn't get discussed enough: the people most responsible for shaping its education system, its policy landscape, and its startup ecosystem rarely speak publicly in a way that lets you understand how they actually think.

You'll catch a quote in an article. A panel soundbite. A carefully worded press release. But the real conversation — the one where someone admits what went wrong, explains the tradeoff they agonised over, or shares the mental model that changed how they work — that almost never makes it out.

"The most useful conversations I've had have never been on stage. I wanted to build a space where the candid version is the default."

I started The Insight Hour to fix that. Every episode is a long-form, unscripted conversation with someone who is genuinely doing interesting work — and who is willing to go beyond the official line.

What the Newsletter Is

Listening to a full-length episode isn't always possible. So after every conversation, I write a detailed newsletter summarising the key ideas, frameworks, and arguments from that episode. You get the substance even if you're short on time.

The newsletter is not a transcript. It's a distillation — the four or five things the guest said that genuinely shifted how I see something, written up so they're useful and portable. Readers tell me the newsletter is often better than the episode, which I take as both a compliment and a challenge.

Who This Is For

If you work in education, policy, or entrepreneurship in India — or care about any of those things — this podcast is for you. I don't assume prior expertise, but I don't dumb things down either. The guests are smart and opinionated, and I try to keep up.

I think the conversations here are most useful for people who want to understand the systems they're working in — not just how to navigate them, but why they work the way they do, and where the real leverage points are.
